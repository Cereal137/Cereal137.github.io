Color Gradients

"Color Gradients Since the Epoch of Reionization"

We study the color gradient in galaxies at 4 < z < 7.5, using the high-resolution imaging data from the JWST Cosmic Evolution Early Release Science (CEERS) program. We select a sample of 270 galaxies with previous multiband measurement including HST. We use GALFITM to simultaneously fit the light profile of these galaxies for better characterization of wavelength dependency of structural parameters. In search of color gradients, we find a neutral distribution of effective radius and Sérsic index variation, which implies a weak dependency of structural parameters on wavelength at high redshift, consistent with the latest JWST results. Interestingly, we find that the variation of effective radius has a pronounced dependency on its sizes: the smallest galaxies tend to establish positive gradients while the more-assembled ones showcase a trend of negative gradients, providing a new observational evidence for the inside-out growth scenario. Combining measurement of Sérsic index, a more compact morphology in rest-frame UV is tentatively discerned for the smallest systems and bluest SEDs.
