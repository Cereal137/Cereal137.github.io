    • Research Interest and Experience

    Galaxy Morphology

    I'm interested in observations, especially in the field of galaxy morphology. I use GALFIT and GALFITM to measure the size and structure of galaxies. I am currently in extragalactic teams analyzing mostly the early data from James Webb Space Telescope (JWST).

    Preliminary results on multiband morphology of high redshift galaxies:

    • Single Sérsic Fit: Multiband fit on 379 high-redshift candidates with GALFITM using 3 NIRCam SW filters F115W, F150W, F200W and 4 LW filters F277W, F356W, F410M, F444W.

    • Measurement of photometric redshift of candidates combining new JWST Near-Infrared data with photometry catalog from Stefanon et al. (2017), Tests on well-cited photo-z code for high-z candidates, SED fitting of stellar mass and SFRs using CIGALE, etc.

    Galaxy Formation and Evolution

    I'm interested in the formation and evolution of galaxies. Observationally, I study how the stellar population of galaxies form and evolve through starlight.

    I've also joined a Dark matter & Galaxies group led by Prof. Fangzhou Jiang, to provide theoretical explanation on how galaxies evolve.

    AGN Physics

    I'm interested in the physics of Active Galactic Necleus, spanning the wavelength coverage from radio far into the high energy spectrum. I'd like to study multiband AGN activity, to seek for constraints on the SMBH behaviour and how it coevolve with its host galaxy.
